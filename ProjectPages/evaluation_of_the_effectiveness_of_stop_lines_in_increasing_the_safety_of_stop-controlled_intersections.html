Evaluation of the Effectiveness of Stop Lines in Increasing the Safety of Stop-Controlled Intersections

October 2011 - October 2012 | by Melissa Duhn, Peter Dirks, Andrew Loutfi, John Hourdos, Gary Davis

Purpose:

This project examined the long-term impact of stop lines on intersection safety and driver behavior through crash data analysis and video observation at 16 intersections. The study found that stop lines did not significantly influence safety or behavior, with factors like speed limits and sight distance being more critical for improving intersection safety and compliance.
